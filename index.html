Famous Missions

Unveiling Legendary Missions That Shaped History

Throughout history, numerous missions have shaped our world, pushing the boundaries of exploration, science, and innovation. From space expeditions like Apollo 11, which landed humans on the Moon

Chandrayaan-1

Launched on October 22, 2008, Chandrayaan-1 was India's first lunar mission. It discovered water molecules in the lunar soil and mapped the Moon's surface, contributing to global lunar science.

Mangalyaan (MOM)

Launched on November 5, 2013, the Mars Orbiter Mission made India the first Asian nation to reach Mars orbit on its first attempt. It studied the Martian atmosphere and surface features.

Chandrayaan-2

Launched on July 22, 2019, Chandrayaan-2 aimed to explore the Moon's south pole. Although the lander failed to soft-land, the orbiter continues to send valuable data about the lunar surface.

Explore the Cosmos

Planets of Our Solar System

Discover the fascinating worlds that orbit our Sun, each with its own unique characteristics and mysteries waiting to be explored.

Mercury

The smallest planet, closest to the Sun, with extreme temperatures and a cratered surface resembling our Moon.

Distance from Sun: 58 million km

Diameter: 4,879 km

Fun Fact: A day on Mercury lasts 59 Earth days, but its year is only 88 Earth days!

Venus

A scorching hot world with thick clouds of sulfuric acid and a runaway greenhouse effect, Earth's "evil twin."

Distance from Sun: 108 million km

Diameter: 12,104 km

Fun Fact: Venus rotates backward compared to most planets, so the Sun rises in the west!

Earth

Our home, the only known planet with life, featuring vast oceans, diverse ecosystems, and a protective atmosphere.

Distance from Sun: 150 million km

Diameter: 12,742 km

Fun Fact: Earth’s atmosphere scatters sunlight, giving us blue skies and stunning sunsets!

Mars

The Red Planet, known for its iron-rich dust and potential for past water flows, inspiring dreams of future colonization.

Distance from Sun: 228 million km

Diameter: 6,792 km

Fun Fact: Mars hosts the solar system’s largest volcano, Olympus Mons, standing 22 km tall!

Jupiter

A gas giant with swirling storms, including the Great Red Spot, and a massive magnetic field dominating its many moons.

Distance from Sun: 778 million km

Diameter: 139,820 km

Fun Fact: Jupiter’s Great Red Spot is a storm that has raged for at least 300 years!

Saturn

Famous for its stunning ring system, this gas giant boasts a delicate beauty and intriguing moons like Titan.

Distance from Sun: 1,429 million km

Diameter: 120,536 km

Fun Fact: Saturn’s rings are made of ice and rock particles, some as big as mountains!

Uranus

An ice giant with a pale blue hue from methane, tilted on its side, and surrounded by faint rings and moons.

Distance from Sun: 2,871 million km

Diameter: 51,118 km

Fun Fact: Uranus is tilted 98 degrees, likely due to a massive collision in its past!

Neptune

A deep blue ice giant with fierce winds and a dynamic atmosphere, home to the mysterious moon Triton.

Distance from Sun: 4,498 million km

Diameter: 49,244 km

Fun Fact: Neptune has the strongest winds in the solar system, reaching speeds of 2,400 km/h!
